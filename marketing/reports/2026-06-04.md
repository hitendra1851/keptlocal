### Thread 2 — Show HN: Pdfwithlove — PDF tools that run 100% locally (no uploads, no back end)
- **URL:** https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46675231
- **Platform:** Hacker News — January 27, 2026
- **Context:** Creator argues cloud-based PDF processing is a structural privacy risk. Discussion covers which WebAssembly PDF libraries are production-ready, whether browser-based tools match Ghostscript compression quality, and what happens to files on iLovePDF's servers. A follow-up post two weeks later noted ~9,500 visitors from the HN post alone.
- **Draft reply for Hiten to post:**
  > The Ghostscript-vs-browser question comes up a lot. For lossless operations like merge, split, rotate, and page extraction, a browser-based pdf-lib approach is indistinguishable from server-side. Where it falls short is heavy compression — Ghostscript's image downsampling genuinely outperforms what you can do in a WASM sandbox today. So the honest answer is: for 80% of what people do with PDFs (combining, reordering, watermarking), a tool like this or keptlocal.com is perfectly sufficient and your file never moves. For "shrink this 50MB scanned PDF to 2MB", you'd still want a local Ghostscript install or accept the trade-off of a server-side tool.

Batch support for 20+ images at once is the next feature people ask for after they've used single-file compression a few times.
